During the French Revolution, Napoleon Bonaparte rose from obscurity to become a prominent military leader. In 1793, he gained fame for recapturing Toulon from the British. In 1795, he defended the revolutionary government against royalist forces in Paris. His successes led to his appointment as commander of the Army of Italy in 1796, where he achieved significant victories. The Revolution provided the backdrop for his ascent to power, culminating in his eventual rule over France.
